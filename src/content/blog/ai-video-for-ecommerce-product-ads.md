Static product photos sell. Product *video* sells more — every platform now ranks and rewards motion, and shoppers consistently convert better when they can see a product move, scale and sit in a real space.

The catch has always been cost: a product video meant a shoot, a set, lighting, and an editor. Small Bridges removes that. If you have a product photo, you have the raw material for an ad. Here's the workflow.

## Start from the image you already have

Drop a clean product shot into the studio and use **image-to-video**. The engines animate from your real product — a rotating hero, a slow push-in, a hand reaching for it — so the thing on screen is unmistakably *yours*, not a generic stand-in.

For products where motion matters (apparel, food, gadgets, anything tactile), reach for **Seedance 2.0** — its hyperreal motion keeps fabric, liquid and reflections believable in slow motion.

## Put it in a world

A product floating on white is fine for a PDP. An ad needs a *place*. The studio ships 120 hand-graded environments — golden-hour kitchens, neon city nights, minimalist studios — so you can stage the same product for different audiences in minutes:

- A skincare bottle in soft morning light for a wellness angle
- The same bottle under punchy studio light for a performance-marketing angle
- It on a bathroom shelf for the "in your life" angle

No reshoot. Just a new environment.

## Add a presenter (optional, but powerful)

UGC-style ads outperform polished ones on social. Cast one of 534 avatars as a presenter, hand them a line, and they deliver it — lip-synced, in their own voice, looking down the lens. You get the "real person talking about the product" format without hiring a creator or filming yourself.

Want the avatar to literally reach out of the frame and hold your product up to the viewer? That's a 4th-wall break, and it's a few clicks.

## Cut for the platform

Different feeds, different rules. The studio exports any aspect ratio:

- **9:16** for TikTok, Reels and Shorts
- **1:1** for the feed
- **16:9** for YouTube pre-roll and your site

Start from a template — viral hook, product demo, satisfying ASMR — and the pacing, captions and music come pre-built. Swap in your shots and you have a platform-native cut.

## A repeatable system, not a one-off

The real win for a store isn't one ad — it's *velocity*. Once your product is in the studio you can spin up:

1. A hero launch film
2. Five short hooks to test
3. A presenter explainer
4. Seasonal re-cuts in new environments

All from the same source photo, all on-brand, all without booking a studio.

## The bottom line

Product video used to be the expensive part of e-commerce. Now it's a sentence and a photo. Upload your best shot, pick a world, and let the studio turn it into something a shopper can't scroll past.
